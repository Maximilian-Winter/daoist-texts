# Preface to the Five Talismans of the Supreme Cavernous Mystery and Spiritual Treasure

Title of the Scripture: Preface to the Five Talismans of the Supreme Cavernous Mystery and Spiritual Treasure. Author unknown, roughly dating from Wei and Jin dynasties. It is one of the ancient Scriptures of the Spiritual Treasure. Ge Hong in the Inner Chapters of the Master Embracing Simplicity has already cited this book. The original was one scroll, later divided into three. Source text: The Zhengtong Daoist Canon, Cavernous Mystery section, category of Divine Talismans.

## Preface to the Five Talismans of the Supreme Cavernous Mystery and Spiritual Treasure, Scroll One

In the profound antiquity there was purity and harmony, yin and yang without distinction; the wind did not move the branches, the clouds and rain came as appointed. All things gave loose rein to their hearts, living beings followed the Way and walked accordingly. Above they faced the heart of Heaven; Heaven and man united their Qi. Good and evil were not yet apparent; none spoke of killing or harming, none feared ill fortune, none rejoiced in good fortune. There was no discourse of right or wrong, no teaching of distinctions; all was simple and unblemished, erect and undiminished. They pushed Heaven and entrusted themselves to their nature, committed to Earth and indulged their destiny; thousands upon thousands obtained their place; none injured the harmony. That harmony did not harm; Heaven and Earth had no lord; without lordship they transformed themselves. The great teaching that does not teach preserves their destiny.

In those times men lived ninety thousand years, then began to learn the way of immortality and ascend to Heaven; this might be called supreme transformation, following the heart, and supreme teaching.
